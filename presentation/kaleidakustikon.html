Waltzing forever. Encoding Friedrich Kuhlau's Kaleidakustikon


Kaleidakustikon

  • Written by Friedrich Kuhlau around 1820
  • An aleatoric composition for piano
  • Consists of 21 piles of cards (names a to v excluding j)
  • Each pile contains 11 cards (except h which contains 3)
  • Each card contains one or two measures
  • Simple three-part structure, in F, F or F minor, and E flat respectively
  • Modern edition exists in Gorm Busk & Jan Maegaard (1996) Kuhlay Kanons
